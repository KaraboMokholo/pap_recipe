Krummel/Phutu pap (Crumbly pap): A dry, crumbly texture prepared by steaming the maize meal, often served with chakalaka (a spicy vegetable relish) or stew

link : https://marriamsayed.com/2020/09/08/traditional-south-african-phuthu-uphuthu-krummel-pap/



Ingredients :
main - Maize meal , Water, Salt
Optional - milk (for softer pap) , butter(for a richer flavour), sugar , honey and lemon juice(for a sweet pap)



instructions:

1. Slap Pap (Soft Porridge)
Slap pap is a smooth, porridge-like consistency, typically enjoyed for breakfast with milk and sugar, or as a side dish. 

Ratio: 1 cup maize meal to 4 cups water. 

Instructions:

Boil water: Bring 4 cups of water and ½ tsp salt to a boil in a pot.

Add maize meal: Gradually whisk in 1 cup of maize meal, stirring continuously to prevent lumps.

Simmer: Reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer for about 15 minutes, stirring often.

Finish: Stir in optional butter and serve warm. 
2. Stywe Pap (Stiff Pap)
Stywe pap is firm and thick, often served with stews or braai (barbecue) meat. 
Ratio: 2 cups maize meal to 2½ cups water. 
Instructions:
Boil water: Bring 2½ cups of water and 1 tsp salt to a boil in a large pot.
Add maize meal: Slowly pour in the maize meal. Do not stir immediately; let it sit on top of the water for a minute, then stir well with a wooden spoon, mashing against the sides to break up lumps.
Steam: Reduce heat to low, cover with a lid, and let it steam for 10-15 minutes.
Stir and finish: Stir well again, cover, and cook for another 10 minutes until firm and cooked through. Serve hot. 
3. Krummelpap / Putu Pap (Crumbly Pap)
Krummelpap is a dry and grainy texture, commonly served with a rich tomato and onion sauce (sheba or chakalaka) and boerewors. 
Ratio: 2.5 parts maize meal to 2 parts water. 
Instructions:
Boil water: Bring 2½ cups of water and salt to a gentle boil in a pot.
Add maize meal: Sprinkle the maize meal evenly over the water, forming a heap in the center. Do not stir.
Steam: Cover the pot tightly with a lid and steam on the lowest heat for 10-20 minutes.
Fluff: Use a fork to gently mix and separate the grains into a crumb-like texture.
Cook further: Cover and steam for another 10-20 minutes, fluffing occasionally with a fork until fully cooked and crumbly.
